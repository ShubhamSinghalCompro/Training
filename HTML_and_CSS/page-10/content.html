So/neither/nor + auxiliary + pronoun


'I've never had any friends,' said Mary sadly.

'Neither have I,' said Ben.

•
If you want to agree with a positive statement, use so + auxiliary + pronoun.

'I like swimming in the sea.'

'So do I.'

'My brother can speak French.'

'So can I.'

•
If you want to agree with a negative statement, use neither/nor + auxiliary + pronoun.

'I don't like working in the garden.'

'Neither do I.'

'She won't go to London.'

'Nor will I.'

Be careful!Remember to use the same auxiliary verb as in the sentence you are responding to.

'He will win a prize.'

'So will I.'
